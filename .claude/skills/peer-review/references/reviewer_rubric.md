# Reviewer Rubric

Use this rubric for external manuscript review. The goal is to evaluate the submitted argument, not to reconstruct the author's intended argument.

## Novelty

- What is new relative to the manuscript's own related-work framing?
- Is the novelty conceptual, methodological, empirical, evaluative, governance-oriented, or presentational?
- Does the manuscript overstate novelty by ignoring adjacent work?

## Significance

- Why does the problem matter?
- Who benefits from the contribution?
- Does the evidence show importance or merely assert it?

## Gap-Contribution Fit

- Is the gap explicit and specific?
- Does each contribution answer that gap?
- Are any contributions only implementation features?
- Are there gaps that are named but not answered?

## Claim-Evidence Adequacy

- What are the paper's main claims?
- What evidence supports each one?
- Is the evidence direct, background, methodological, workflow, governance, abstract-only, or candidate-only?
- Are any claims under-supported, over-cited, or supported by misaligned evidence?

## Method And Evaluation

- Are data, methods, baselines, metrics, denominators, and failure cases clear?
- Does the evaluation match the claim level?
- Are ablations, controls, comparisons, or qualitative analyses missing?

## Overclaim And Generalisation

- Does the manuscript imply causal, deployment, outcome, or field-wide validity beyond its evidence?
- Are limitations explicit and tied to the claims they constrain?
- Are claims framed with appropriate boundary language?

## Reproducibility And Transparency

- Are artifacts, data, code, prompts, annotation rules, or provenance details sufficient for the stated contribution?
- Are generated or agent-assisted materials separated from verified evidence?

## Structure And Writing

- Can the reviewer state the paper's contribution after reading the abstract and introduction?
- Do figures and tables carry the argument?
- Are results presented as narrative evidence rather than raw inventory?
