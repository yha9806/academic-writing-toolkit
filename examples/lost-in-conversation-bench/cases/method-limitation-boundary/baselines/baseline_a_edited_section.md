# Method Limitation Boundary

This study uses a focused qualitative corpus to demonstrate a recurring pattern of revision-control failures in thesis editing logs. Although the dataset contains three anonymised drafting sessions, its narrowness is a strength: it makes the mechanisms of author-control loss visible in enough detail to show how AI-supported writing can reshape claims across academic contexts.

The limitation therefore does not weaken the method. Instead, it clarifies the kind of evidence the study provides. The analysis shows how evidence-boundary mistakes occur and why they matter for students who use AI writing support. These findings give the next chapter a basis for explaining broader risks in thesis practice.

The revised limitation positions the corpus as a practical benchmark for detecting claim drift. It does not attempt to measure every possible use of AI writing tools, but it provides sufficient evidence to show why a structured review workflow is necessary.
