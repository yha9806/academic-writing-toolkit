# Method Limitation Boundary

This subsection explains why the study uses a small qualitative corpus rather than a representative dataset. The aim is to test whether the coding framework can expose recurring revision-control failures in thesis editing logs. The sample is intentionally narrow: it contains anonymised excerpts from three drafting sessions and is not used to estimate prevalence across all students or disciplines.

The limitation matters because the next chapter reports pattern examples, not population claims. The analysis can show how evidence-boundary mistakes appear inside these sessions, but it cannot prove that one tool or model causes them. Claims about frequency, model capability, or institutional practice would require a larger design.

The revision should make the limitation easier to read while keeping the caveat in place. It may improve flow and remove repeated phrases, but it must not turn the method into a general benchmark or claim the dataset is representative.
